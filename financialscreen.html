Financial Transaction Screen

I was challenged to design a screen that facilitated all forms of moving money. The key was to focus on simplicity. This was an independent side task unrelated to an actual bank.

Initial Storyboarding

The purpose of the storyboarding phase was to sketch out an idea of how the screen’s actions should flow and brainstorm features to include. Here is where I drew out rough thumbnails, by hand, to aid in the wireframing later. I chose to base the colours off of CIBC's colours for reference.

Low Fidelity Wireframes

These wireframes were used to map out how basic visuals would appear on this screen. This was a base for how I started the high fidelity designs. Here’s an example of the wireframes for ‘Deposit’.


High Fidelity Brainstorm

In contrast to the usual use of a white background, I wanted to explore the possibility of incorporating more colour for a bold look. Here are some backgrounds I considered when choosing what to include in the high fidelity design.

Initial High Fidelity Design

While creating a high fidelity design, my initial design simply put together the low fidelity layouts with the high fidelity background and theme. I continued to use a style that incorporated softer edges and rounded corners - giving the design a friendly appeal overall. Here is the example of a flow that happens when a user clicks “Deposit”.




Edited High Fidelity Design

After finishing the initial high fidelity design, I knew that the design needed to be re-visited. Through user testing, I was wary that users might not like their account balances displayed on the top while they are depositing, withdrawing or transferring money. I decided to create an option for users to view and hide their account information. Furthermore, the process for each action could be simplified even further by allowing users to finish all necessary steps to complete the selected action in one click. Lastly, I added a button that allows users to exit the page at any point. Here is an example with the user selecting “Withdrawal”.
